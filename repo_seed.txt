# kanizsa_shapes

> pac-man sectors. missing pieces. the brain draws the triangle that isn't there. kanizsa illusory contours. parameterize the inducers: rotation, gap size, contrast. watch the illusion strengthen and fade.

**Archetype:** SURFACE. **Distinct from** `rotating_snakes` (motion illusion, not contour completion) and `simultaneous_contrast` (color, not form).

## Adjacency
- `sacred_geometry` — geometric construction, not illusory
- `glass_patterns` — global form from local cues, similar mechanism
- `simultaneous_contrast` — color interaction, not contour completion

## Visual Targets
- Kanizsa triangle: 3 pac-man inducers pointing inward
- Kanizsa square: 4 inducers, square illusion
- Subjective surfaces: illusory brightness within the perceived shape
- Inducer rotation: spinning pac-man sectors
- Gap size: smaller gap = stronger illusion
- Contrast: high contrast = stronger illusion
- Figure-ground: ambiguous vase/faces style arrangements

## Technical Notes
- Illusory contours: V2 neurons respond to the non-existent edge
- Subjective brightness: the illusory shape appears brighter than background
- Local contour completion: brain fills gaps based on good continuation
- Global shape perception: top-down influence of expected shape
